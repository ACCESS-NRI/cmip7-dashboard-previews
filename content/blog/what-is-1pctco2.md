**1pctCO2** is an idealised ramp experiment. Starting from the pre-industrial
control climate, the carbon dioxide concentration is increased by **1% every
year**, compounding, so it doubles after about 70 years and quadruples after
roughly 140.

## What it does

Like `abrupt-4xCO2`, this run is not meant to be realistic — no real-world
scenario raises CO2 on such a smooth, steady schedule. Instead of one instant
shock, though, `1pctCO2` applies the CO2 as a slow, clean ramp. That lets us
watch the climate respond to a *gradually* strengthening
:jargon[forcing]{term="forcing"}, which is much closer to how the real world
warms over time.

## Why we care

The steady ramp is what makes this experiment so useful:

- **Transient Climate Response (TCR)** — the warming at the moment CO2 doubles
  (around year 70) tells us how much the planet heats up while the ocean is
  still catching up.
- **:jargon[TCRE]{term="TCRE"}** — because emissions accumulate smoothly, this
  run is the backbone for relating cumulative CO2 to warming, which underpins
  carbon budgets.
- It bridges the gap between the instantaneous `abrupt-4xCO2` shock and the
  messier, forcing-rich `historical` run.

`1pctCO2` is one of the core :jargon[DECK]{term="DECK"} experiments that every
:jargon[CMIP]{term="CMIP"} model runs, so its results can be compared cleanly
from one model to the next.
