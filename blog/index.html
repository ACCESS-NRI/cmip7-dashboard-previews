What is the 1pctCO2 experiment?
CO2 rises by 1% every year until it has quadrupled — a clean, gradual ramp used to measure how fast the climate warms.
8 July 2026 · Charles Turner, ACCESS-NRI
What is the 1pctCO2-bgc experiment?
The same 1%-a-year CO2 ramp, but only the carbon cycle 'sees' it — isolating how much carbon the land and ocean soak up.
8 July 2026 · Charles Turner, ACCESS-NRI
What is the 1pctCO2-rad experiment?
The 1%-a-year CO2 ramp is felt only by the radiation code — isolating how a warming climate weakens natural carbon sinks.
4 July 2026 · Charles Turner, ACCESS-NRI
What is the abrupt-4xCO2 experiment?
A deliberately extreme idealised run: quadruple the CO2 instantly and watch how the climate responds.
3 July 2026 · Charles Turner, ACCESS-NRI
What is the historical experiment?
The historical run recreates the climate of 1850 to the present using our best estimate of what actually drove it.
2 July 2026 · Charles Turner, ACCESS-NRI
What is the piControl experiment?
A long, stable simulation of a pre-industrial world that acts as the quiet baseline for every other run.
